Surgery for breast cancer is often the last resort method to save patients but the success rates of surgery itself is questionable as women have been recorded dying post-surgery within 5 years of the operation in University of Chicago's Billings Hospital. This project was aimed at building a model that can predict the survival of a breast cancer patient post-surgery using key measures like number of axillary nodes found in the body and the age at which diagnosis was done.
